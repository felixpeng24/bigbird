# Shopping Specs: Big Bird is Watching You

## Overview

I am building an interactive art installation using a Raspberry Pi 5 inside a Big Bird puppet. I need to buy the components listed below. I already own the Raspberry Pi 5 and its accessories (camera module, active cooler, power supply, microSD card). I need everything else.

**Total Budget:** $333 (somewhat flexible, slightly over is okay)
**Shipping:** I am located in the Claremont, California area. Amazon Prime is preferred. Other trusted sellers are fine (Adafruit, SparkFun, Walmart, Target, etc.).
**Timeline:** No rush. Preferably everything arrives within one week of ordering.

---

## Items Needed

### 1. Big Bird Plush / Stuffed Animal

**What I need:** A large Big Bird stuffed animal or puppet. It needs to be big enough to fit a Raspberry Pi 5, a small speaker, and cable routing inside its body. I will be cutting into it to mount a camera in one eye and hide electronics inside.

**Key requirements:**
- At least 2 feet tall, bigger is better
- Does NOT need to be official Sesame Street, premium, or licensed. Just needs to somewhat resemble Big Bird (big yellow bird). A generic or off-brand large yellow bird plush is totally fine.
- Soft/plush body that I can cut open and re-stitch or tape closed
- Cheapest option that meets the size requirement

**Quantity:** 1

**Budget range:** $20-60 (flexible if a great large one is slightly more)

---

### 2. Small HDMI Display (5-7 inches)

**What I need:** A small HDMI monitor that works with the Raspberry Pi 5. Mounted on or next to the puppet to display the user interface. Does NOT need to be a touchscreen.

**Key requirements:**
- 7 inch preferred (larger = more readable from a few feet away, lets nearby observers watch too). 5 inch is acceptable if 7 inch options are too expensive.
- HDMI input (compatible with Raspberry Pi 5 via micro-HDMI to HDMI)
- Powered via USB (from the Pi or a separate USB power source, no separate wall plug)
- At least 1024x600 resolution preferred. 800x480 is acceptable minimum.
- IPS panel preferred over TN (better viewing angles — observers standing to the side can still read the screen)
- Comes with a stand or has mounting options
- Does NOT need touch capability
- Confirmed Raspberry Pi 5 compatible

**Quantity:** 1

**Budget range:** $25-50 (flexible, 7 inch IPS panels may run $35-50)

---

### 3. Big Red Arcade Button

**What I need:** A large, satisfying, arcade-style push button. Red. This is what participants press to start the evaluation. Wired to the Pi's GPIO pins.

**Key requirements:**
- Large, at least 60mm diameter. 100mm (4 inches) is ideal. Go big.
- Red color
- Momentary push button (NOT toggle or latching)
- Standard NO (normally open) contacts with wireable terminals (just two wires needed)
- Built-in LED backlight is a nice bonus but not required
- Comes with mounting hardware (nut/washer for panel mounting)

**Quantity:** 2 (one to use, one backup in case the first breaks or has issues)

**Budget range:** $5-15 each

---

### 4. Small Speaker

**What I need:** A small speaker that fits inside the puppet's throat/chest area. Plays robotic text-to-speech from the Pi. Connects via 3.5mm audio jack or USB.

**Key requirements:**
- Small enough to fit inside a stuffed animal (roughly 2-4 inches)
- Powered via USB (from the Pi or its own battery)
- 3.5mm aux input OR USB audio (either works, 3.5mm is simpler)
- Loud enough for a gallery/hallway setting (clearly audible from 3-5 feet away)
- Clear speech reproduction (does not need bass, just clear vocals)
- Works with Raspberry Pi out of the box, no special drivers

**Quantity:** 1 (plus note a backup option in case this one is too quiet or doesn't fit)

**Budget range:** $8-20

---

### 5. Cables and Wiring

**Micro-HDMI to HDMI cable:**
- The Raspberry Pi 5 has micro-HDMI output. Need a cable to connect to the HDMI display.
- Short length is fine (1-3 feet)
- Quantity: 1

**Female-to-female jumper wires:**
- For connecting the arcade button to the Pi's GPIO pins
- Need at least 8-12 inches (20-30cm) length so they can route from the Pi inside the puppet to the button outside
- Quantity: 1 small pack (usually comes in 10-40 wire packs, that is fine, extras are useful)

**3.5mm audio cable (short):**
- To connect Pi's headphone jack to the speaker (only needed if the speaker uses 3.5mm input)
- 1-2 feet is enough
- Quantity: 1

**Pi Camera ribbon cable extension (15-pin, 22-pin, or compatible with Camera Module 3):**
- The stock camera ribbon cable is ~15cm. The camera needs to route from the Pi board (inside the puppet body) up to the eye socket, which may be 30-50cm away depending on puppet size.
- 30cm or 50cm extension or replacement cable
- Must be compatible with Pi 5 + Camera Module 3 (Pi 5 uses a smaller 22-pin FPC connector, NOT the old 15-pin)
- Quantity: 1

**USB-C right-angle adapter or short extension:**
- The Pi will be enclosed inside the puppet. A right-angle USB-C adapter makes it easier to route the power cable out cleanly without stressing the connector.
- Quantity: 1 (optional but recommended)

**Budget range:** $15-30 total for all cables

---

### 6. Button Mounting Surface

**What I need:** The arcade button needs to be mounted in or on something. It is a panel-mount button, meaning you drill a hole in a flat surface and the button sits in the hole with a nut on the back. I need something to serve as a podium or base for the button.

**Options (recommend the cheapest/simplest):**
- A small wooden box or craft box from a craft store
- A small plastic project enclosure
- A thick piece of wood or MDF that can sit on a table
- Anything flat and sturdy with enough surface area to drill/cut a hole for the button

**Quantity:** 1

**Budget range:** $5-15

---

### 7. Power Strip / Extension Cord

**What I need:** At the installation venue, the nearest outlet may not be right next to the puppet. I need a short extension cord or power strip to run power to the Pi and potentially the display.

**Key requirements:**
- Standard US outlet
- At least 6 feet of cord
- Power strip with 3+ outlets is preferred over a single extension cord (Pi power supply + display USB power + any extras)

**Quantity:** 1

**Budget range:** $5-15
